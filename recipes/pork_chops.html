French Onion braised pork chops

Description

This recipe for French Onion Braised Pork Chops combines the savory, comforting elements of classic French onion soup with juicy, pan-seared pork. The dish features thick-cut pork chops that are seared until golden and then braised in a rich, beefy gravy made with deeply caramelized onions and aromatic herbs. Topped with a blanket of melted Gruyère or Swiss cheese, it is a hearty one-pan meal that balances the sweetness of the onions with the richness of the cheese and savory meat.

Ingredients

  • 4 to 5 pork chops (bone-in or boneless, about 1 inch thick)
  • 2 large yellow or white onions, thinly sliced into rings
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil (plus more if needed)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup beef broth (low sodium preferred)
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme or 1 tablespoon fresh thyme
  • ½ teaspoon dried rosemary or 1 teaspoon fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 4 to 5 slices of Gruyère, Swiss, or Provolone cheese
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Optional: Garlic powder for seasoning the chops

Steps

  1. Preheat and Prep: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Pat the pork chops dry with paper towels and season both sides generously with salt, pepper, and garlic powder if using.
  2. Caramelize the Onions: In a large, oven-safe skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and cook, stirring frequently, for about 15–20 minutes until they are soft, golden brown, and caramelized. Add the minced garlic during the last minute of cooking. Remove the onions from the skillet and set them aside on a plate.
  3. Sear the Pork: Increase the skillet heat to medium-high and add a drizzle of olive oil. Sear the pork chops for 2–3 minutes per side until a golden-brown crust forms. Remove the chops and set them aside.
  4. Make the Gravy: Return the caramelized onions to the skillet over medium heat. Sprinkle the flour over the onions and stir for about 1 minute to cook out the raw flour taste. Slowly pour in the beef broth while whisking or stirring constantly to prevent lumps. Add the thyme and rosemary.
  5. Simmer and Braise: Bring the sauce to a gentle simmer for 2–3 minutes until it begins to thicken. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper. Nestle the seared pork chops back into the skillet, spooning some of the onion gravy over the top of each chop.
  6. Bake: Place a slice of cheese over each pork chop. Transfer the skillet to the oven and bake for 10–15 minutes, or until the pork reaches an internal temperature of 145°F and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  7. Serve: Remove from the oven and let the meat rest for 5 minutes before serving. Spoon the remaining onion gravy from the pan over the chops.
